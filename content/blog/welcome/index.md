Something that we learned after Storm Bert was that we seemed to have lost our focus on flood preparedness. People had moved out of the area, and with them, a bit of local knowledge and coordination was gone too.

We've found that the more we achieve for the community, the more we need to communicate and share.

This website is a set of resources and a blog that should help more communities start and run a Flood Action Group.

We've also built a separate site for local river levels, to help people in rural areas know whether flooding is likely.
The FLAG started out sharing information primarily via a chat channel, then the village Facebook group, and later, the Parish newsletter.

We think we can do things even better by having our own website with information that helps the community, so that it's always there when they need it. It also gives us a little more flexibility and empowerment, not reliant on a social media platform, and supplementing not overloading the Parish website.

We think having this website helps us "bake in" the knowledge we have and volunteering that we do, making it less likely that it drifts away again. We think this increases our community resilience. We also think we can share our approach and what we've learned with other communities too.
