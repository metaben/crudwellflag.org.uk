After the last major flood, some questions came up:

1. how deep was the flooding?
2. when do we need to put to my flood barriers up?
3. how will we know how bad things are getting if it happens at night?

Our short-term fix was to persuade the Parish Council to buy and install a depth board in the river, which they have kindly done. Anybody can see the board from the river bank, although it's not a lot of fun walking out there when it's raining. In a flood event, it won't be safe to go and look at this board.

The Environment Agency has some **[river level monitoring](https://check-for-flooding.service.gov.uk/river-and-sea-levels/crudwell-malmesbury-wiltshire)** with the nearest a few miles downstream at Oaksey, after several water sources have joined the Swill Brook. It lags behind the actual depth here in Crudwell.

We have built a new River Levels website which displays river depths from multiple sensors. We want to develop this into a simple tool that helps communities know the state of flooding on a local and even hyperlocal basis.
