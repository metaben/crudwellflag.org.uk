After the last major flood, some questions came up:

1. how deep was the flooding?
2. when do we need to put to my flood barriers up?
3. how will we know how bad things are getting if it happens at night?

Our short-term fix was to persuade the Parish Council to buy and install a depth board in the river, which they have kindly done. Anybody can see the board from the river bank, although it's not a lot of fun walking out there when it's raining. In a flood event, it won't be safe to go and look at this board.

The Environment Agency has some **[river level monitoring](https://check-for-flooding.service.gov.uk/river-and-sea-levels/crudwell-malmesbury-wiltshire)** with the nearest a few miles downstream at Oaksey, after other water sources have joined the Swill Brook. It lags behind the actual depth here in Crudwell. How is a resident supposed to know that "1.10m" river depth several miles downstream, means there will be imminent flooding in Crudwell? It's a useful source of data but there's still an element of local knowledge as well as watching the weather.

We'd like to know exactly what river level means flooding. And we'd like to know this as local to us as possible, that would be better than several miles downstream.

We have built and deployed our own sensor, and we have also begun building a new River Levels website - to display river depths from multiple local sensors. We think we can develop this into a simple tool that helps communities know the state of flooding on a local or even hyperlocal basis.
